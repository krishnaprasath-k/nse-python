# Institutional-Style Short-Term Trading Framework
*Model-1 → Model-2 → Model-3 Execution System*

This framework identifies short-term trading opportunities (2–5 days) by combining fundamental demand triggers with technical timing and disciplined entries.

The system is structured in three layers:
- **Model-1:** Identify stocks with real demand catalysts
- **Model-2:** Confirm technical timing and structure
- **Model-3:** Execute only when both align with pullback discipline

---

## MODEL-1 — Demand Identification
*(WHAT to Trade)*

Model-1 identifies stocks where new demand or policy catalysts are likely to move price in the next few days.

### Time Horizon
- Short-term trading window: 5–9 trading days

### 1. Industry-First Approach

Start with industries where capital flow or policy catalysts exist.

Primary sectors to monitor:
- Semiconductor / Electronics Manufacturing (EMS)
- Metals (Copper, Steel, Aluminium)
- Power & Energy
- Defence
- Fertilizers & Agro inputs
- Infrastructure / Water / Irrigation
- Oil & Gas (like Adani Gas)

These sectors often respond quickly to policy announcements and economic changes.

### 2. Demand Type Classification

Every stock must fit one clear demand category.

#### Urgent / Forced Demand
Demand that cannot be postponed.

Examples:
- Power generation demand
- Fertilizer demand during crop seasons
- Energy supply requirements

#### Policy-Backed Demand
Demand created by government spending or regulation.

Examples:
- Budget allocations
- PLI schemes
- Defence procurement
- Infrastructure spending
- Renewable energy programs

#### Commodity Cycle Demand
Demand created by global supply-demand imbalances.

Examples:
- Copper shortages
- Steel inventory restocking
- Energy price cycles

#### Seasonal Demand
Demand driven by predictable annual cycles.

Examples:
- Agricultural input demand
- Energy consumption cycles
- Infrastructure spending windows

### 3. Fundamental Filters

Stocks must also meet these criteria:
- Predictable business model
- Visible earnings or cash flow
- Not excessively overvalued
- Active participation from institutional investors
- Sector strength relative to the broader market

**Avoid:**
- Speculative momentum stocks
- Extremely high-PE narrative names

### 4. Demand Confirmation from Market Information

Use information sources such as:
- Policy announcements
- Government budgets
- Sector-specific news
- Commodity trends
- Institutional commentary

This ensures the demand story is real, not speculative.

## MODEL-2 — Technical Timing
*(WHEN to Trade)*

Model-2 confirms that buyers are actually entering the stock.

Technical signals must appear inside a demand zone identified in Model-1.

### 1. Pullback Requirement *(Critical Rule)*

Stocks must be bought only after a pullback into a demand zone.

Acceptable pullback zones:
- Previous breakout level
- 20-EMA or 50-EMA support
- VWAP support (intraday)
- High-volume demand area

**Avoid:**
- Buying at fresh highs
- Chasing vertical candles
- Buying extended gap-ups

### 2. Price Structure Requirements

Valid technical structure includes:
- Higher-low pattern
- W-pattern or double bottom
- Bullish rejection candle
- Stabilization after pullback

These patterns show seller exhaustion and buyer control.

### 3. Momentum Confirmation

Indicators used:
- RSI between 50 and 60
- RSI turning upward
- Rising momentum after pullback

This indicates trend continuation rather than late entry.

### 4. Volume Confirmation

Look for:
- Decreasing volume during pullback
- Increasing volume during the rebound

This shows institutional accumulation.

---

## MODEL-3 — Trade Execution
*(WHEN ALL CONDITIONS ALIGN)*

Model-3 is activated only when: **Model-1 + Model-2 + Market Conditions align**

This stage converts analysis into actual trades.

### 1. Market Regime Filter (Model-0)

Before entering any trade:
- Index must be stable or rising
- Market breadth should not be extremely negative
- No sharp selling pressure in major indices

If the market is weak, skip trading entirely.

### 2. Entry Timing

Enter only when:
- Stock reacts positively from demand zone
- Bullish candle closes above pullback structure
- Volume increases

**Never enter during:**
- First 15 minutes of market
- Uncontrolled gap-ups
- Sharp vertical rallies

### 3. Position Management

Capital allocation example:
- Total capital: ₹1,00,000
- Position size: ₹25,000 per stock
- Maximum 4 stocks simultaneously

### 4. Risk Management Rules

Stop-loss discipline:
- Maximum loss per trade: 2%
- Profit target: 4–5%
- At 3% profit → move stop-loss to cost

> If two stop-losses occur in one session: **Stop trading for the day.**

### 5. Gap-Up Management

If a stock opens more than 2% above the previous close — **do not buy immediately.**

Options:
- Wait for pullback to VWAP
- Wait for gap fill
- Replace with another stock from the watchlist

## Daily Execution Workflow

**Step 1 – Build Model-1 Watchlist**
Identify 10–15 stocks with demand catalysts.

**Step 2 – Filter with Model-2**
Select stocks that:
- Pull back to demand zones
- Show bullish structure
- Display improving momentum (e.g. above 21-day EMA)

**Step 3 – Execute Model-3 Trades**
Choose maximum 4 stocks that meet all conditions. Enter only after technical confirmation.

## Key Philosophy of the System

> *Professional traders buy pullbacks in stocks where demand already exists.*

The system **avoids:**
- Emotional trading
- Chasing momentum
- Trading without catalysts

Instead, it **focuses on:**
- Demand-driven price movement
- Controlled risk
- Repeatable setups

### Core Principle

> Demand creates opportunity.  
> Pullbacks create entry.  
> Confirmation creates trades.

## MODEL-4 — Bearish Market Opportunity Model
*(WHEN MARKETS ARE FALLING)*

Model-4 identifies stocks likely to fall faster than the index when markets enter a risk-off regime.

It can be used for:
- Short selling
- Avoiding weak stocks
- Selecting stocks for intraday downside trades
- Identifying sectors under distribution

### 1. Market Regime Trigger (Activation)

Model-4 activates only when market weakness is confirmed.

**Global Market Signals:**
- US markets falling (S&P 500 / Nasdaq down)
- Asian markets weak
- Global risk-off sentiment

**Domestic Market Signals:**
- Nifty below VWAP
- Nifty making lower highs and lower lows
- Advance/Decline ratio < 0.7
- Banking sector weak

If these occur together → Model-4 becomes active.

### 2. Sector Weakness Identification

Focus only on sectors showing capital outflows.

Typical weak sectors in falling markets:
- High-beta metals
- Midcap infrastructure
- PSU momentum stocks
- Speculative small caps
- Overextended renewable energy stocks

**Avoid defensive sectors like:**
- FMCG
- Pharma
- Utilities

These tend to fall less.

### 3. Weak Stock Characteristics

Stocks selected must show structural weakness.

**Structural Signals:**
- Lower-high pattern
- Breakdown below support
- Price below 20-EMA and 50-EMA
- Failed pullback

**Momentum Signals:**
- RSI below 45
- Increasing volume on down days
- Weak bounce compared to index

These indicate institutional distribution.

### 4. Failed Pullback Setup *(Key Model-4 Entry)*

This is the opposite of Model-3. Instead of buying pullbacks, Model-4 sells failed pullbacks.

**Pattern:**
1. Stock breaks support
2. Small bounce occurs
3. Bounce fails near resistance
4. Downtrend resumes

This is called **bearish continuation**.

### 5. Entry Rules

Short entry only when:
- Stock fails near 20-EMA / VWAP
- Bearish candle forms
- Volume increases

**Never short at extreme lows.** Wait for pullback failure.

### 6. Risk Management

- Stop-loss: 2% above entry
- Target: 4–5% downside
- If market stabilizes → exit early

### 7. Model-4 Execution Logic

Daily workflow:
1. Check global markets
2. Confirm Nifty weakness
3. Identify weak sectors
4. Select stocks breaking support
5. Wait for failed pullback
6. Enter short

### 8. When NOT to Use Model-4

Do not activate Model-4 if:
- Market is sideways
- Index stabilizes above VWAP
- Strong policy news appears
- High institutional buying visible

Model-4 works only in **clear bearish regimes**.
